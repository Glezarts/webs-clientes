Claire Saffitz

Claire Saffitz (born 1986) is an American pastry chef, food writer, and YouTube personality. Before leaving in mid-2020, she was a contributing editor at Bon Appétit magazine and starred in several series on the Bon Appétit YouTube channel, most notably Gourmet Makes, in which she created gourmet versions of popular snack foods by reverse engineering them. She has since published a cookbook titled Dessert Person, which became a New York Times Best Seller, and continues work as a video host on her own YouTube channel and as a freelance recipe developer.

Saffitz was born in 1986 in St. Louis, Missouri, to an Ashkenazi Jewish family. She attended Clayton High School, graduating in 2005. She then graduated from Harvard University in 2009 with a BA in U.S. history and literature, after which she studied French cuisine and pastry at École Grégoire-Ferrandi in Paris, France. After working at Spring Restaurant for a four-month externship, Saffitz moved to Montreal, Québec, where she received a master's degree in History at McGill University in 2013, with a focus on French culinary history in the early modern era.
